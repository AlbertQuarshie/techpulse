Facts and Questions

If you have any problems concerning anything about our website whether functionality-wise or topic-wise, consult the FAQs here.

How do you verify the accuracy of your tech news?

Our editorial team cross-references every story with multiple primary sources. For leaks or rumors, we clearly label the certainty level and provide context from verified historical data.

How do you handle corrections?

Transparency is key. If we make a mistake, we update the article immediately and include a "Correction" note at the bottom or top of the post explaining what was changed and why.

Can I pay for a positive review?

Absolutely not. Our editorial team and our sales team operate completely independently. A "Review" reflects our honest testing; a "Sponsored Post" is clearly marked as paid content.

How do you test products for reviews?

We don't just read spec sheets. Our reviewers use devices as their "daily drivers" for at least a week to test battery life, software stability, and real-world durability before giving a final score.

How can I cite your articles?

You are welcome to quote our work for academic or journalistic purposes, provided you include a clear backlink to the original article and attribute it to TechPulse.
